# How to Give Design Feedback

Effective design feedback is constructive, objective, and actionable. Follow these rules when delivering a critique:

1. **Be Specific:** Avoid vague statements like "The layout is confusing." Instead, pinpoint the issue: "The primary CTA competes with the secondary navigation links."
2. **Explain Why:** Connect your feedback to established design principles, usability heuristics, or user needs. Don't just state an opinion.
3. **Suggest Alternatives:** Don't merely identify problems; propose potential solutions or alternative approaches to explore.
4. **Acknowledge What Works:** Good feedback is balanced. Always include positive observations and validate strong design decisions.
5. **Match the Stage:** Tailor your feedback to the current phase of the design. Early explorations need conceptual and structural feedback; final polish requires pixel-perfect and consistency reviews.
