# Design Critique Framework

Use this structured framework to systematically evaluate designs.

## 1. First Impression (2 seconds)
- What draws the eye first? Is that the intended focal point?
- What is the immediate emotional reaction or vibe?
- Is the purpose of the screen or component immediately clear?

## 2. Usability
- Can the user easily accomplish their primary goal?
- Is the navigation intuitive and predictable?
- Are interactive elements (buttons, links, inputs) clearly identifiable as interactive?
- Are there unnecessary steps, friction points, or cognitive overload?

## 3. Visual Hierarchy
- Is there a clear and logical reading order?
- Are the most important elements emphasized appropriately?
- Is whitespace (negative space) used effectively to group related items and provide breathing room?
- Is the typography scale creating the right hierarchy between headings, body text, and metadata?

## 4. Consistency
- Does the design follow established design system patterns and components?
- Are spacing, colors, and typography used consistently throughout?
- Do similar elements behave and look similarly?

## 5. Accessibility (A11y)
- Are color contrast ratios sufficient for readability (WCAG standards)?
- Are touch target sizes large enough (typically 44x44pt minimum for mobile)?
- Is the text readable in terms of size, line height, and font choice?
- Have considerations been made for alternative text on images and screen reader flow?
