# Voice and Tone Guidelines

**Voice** is your brand's personality—it should remain consistent.
**Tone** is the emotional inflection applied to the voice—it adapts to the user's context.

## Tone Spectrums

### 1. Success & Onboarding (Celebratory / Encouraging)
- **Context:** The user just completed a task, reached a milestone, or signed up.
- **Tone:** Enthusiastic, positive, welcoming.
- **Example:** "You're all set! Let's explore your new dashboard."

### 2. Errors & Failures (Empathetic / Helpful)
- **Context:** Something went wrong (system error, validation error, payment failure).
- **Tone:** Calm, reassuring, direct, and solution-oriented. Never robotic or blaming.
- **Example:** "We couldn't save your changes because the server timed out. Please try again in a moment."

### 3. Warnings & Destructive Actions (Clear / Direct)
- **Context:** The user is about to delete data, change permissions, or take a high-risk action.
- **Tone:** Serious, unambiguous, informative.
- **Example:** "This will permanently delete your workspace and all associated data."

### 4. General Information & Navigation (Neutral / Concise)
- **Context:** Menus, settings, informative tooltips.
- **Tone:** Invisible, efficient, clear.
- **Example:** "Manage billing" or "Notification preferences".
